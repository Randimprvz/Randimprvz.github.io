Let's add opacity to gnome text

After a fair detour i've just learned that opacity isnt real and my mind is blown.

What I mean by this is that the background image you see behind your text, is not continuous with the the rest of the background image but is in fact retroactively created.

For example, black text on a white background will produce gray. But lighten that image enough and you will begin to see the white underneath. However what you are actually seeing is just your letters, there is no 'light shining through' so to speak.

In effect opacity is functioning as a map from opacity percentages to certain mixture ratios between the background and foreground image.

The criterion for whether implementing opacity was viable was whether or not the data structures used to render text included an alpha channel.

In tech speak, an alpha channel is a fancy name for opacity.
